SUN-1 · Sugar Addiction Recovery

Tracker data wasn't reachable from this morning's run (network egress not configured), so this section uses the general framing rather than week-specific notes.

Sunday is the hinge day. What you set up today — groceries in the fridge, one cooked thing on the shelf, one or two protein-forward snacks within arm's reach — decides what Tuesday afternoon's tired brain will reach for. The reach itself is the addiction; the choice is made hours before the craving.

The loop, named plainly

Sugar or a refined-carb shortcut → 30-minute spike → crash → "I have no energy, I can't do this" → reach for the same shortcut. Dr. Anna Lembke calls this dopamine debt: every spike borrows from tomorrow's baseline. The way out isn't willpower — it's removing the gap between hunger and a real food being within reach. Dopamine Nation overview.

Three things to do today

  • Cook one protein anchor. A pan of salmon, a sheet of chicken thighs, or a pot of lentils. One thing that exists in the fridge means Tuesday's tired version of you isn't deciding from scratch.
  • Pre-portion two snacks. Roasted chickpeas, hard-boiled eggs, cottage cheese with hot sauce, edamame. Pre-bagged or pre-bowled. The whole barrier you're removing is "thinking about food when you have no energy to think."
  • Buy one bottle of seltzer with a real flavor (lime, grapefruit). Sober brain still wants a ritual after dinner. Carbonation + something cold + a hand-feel can fill the slot a sweet would.
Today's nudge You don't have to feel motivated to chop vegetables. You just have to put on music and chop the vegetables. The energy comes after the doing, not before — that's the order of operations your brain currently has backwards.
Mantra: "I'm cooking for the person I'll be on Wednesday at 3pm."

SUN-4 · Recipes: Meal Prep

Five recipes designed for one cook session → multiple meals. All low/no added sugar, slow carbs only, protein-forward, no white rice/bread/pasta. Salmon-and-cottage-cheese-heavy because both pull double duty for the fatty liver / pre-diabetes side.

Breakfast — Cottage Cheese Egg Muffins

iFoodReal · Recipe → · ~12 muffins (serves 4–6)

Savory egg muffins baked in a tin. Eat 2–3 cold from the fridge any morning of the week.

Ingredients to shop
  • Large eggs · 2% cottage cheese · red bell pepper · fresh spinach · shredded cheddar · salt · black pepper
  • Heat add: feta + red pepper flakes

Lunch — Mediterranean Lentil & Grain Bowls

Rainbow Plant Life · Recipe → · serves 4–6

Vegan bowls layering spiced French lentils + farro with homemade hummus and quick-pickled onions. Skip the maple in the pickle to keep zero added sugar.

Ingredients to shop
  • French green lentils · farro (or quinoa) · canned chickpeas · vegetable broth · tahini · lemon · garlic · cumin · tamari · red pepper flakes · red onion · apple cider vinegar · olive oil · fresh dill · cherry tomatoes · cucumber · baby spinach/arugula · sumac · salt · black pepper

Dinner — Salmon Meal Prep Bowls

Eating Bird Food · Recipe → · serves 4

Mustard-glazed salmon over quinoa, with roasted sweet potatoes and broccolini, lemon vinaigrette. Add harissa or cayenne to the glaze for heat; use the maple sparingly or skip.

Ingredients to shop
  • Salmon filets · quinoa · sweet potatoes · broccolini · Dijon mustard · garlic · lemon · olive oil · maple syrup (optional) · salt · black pepper · fresh parsley

Snack #1 — Roasted Chickpeas & Edamame

This Wife Cooks · Recipe → · ~4 cups (serves 4–6)

Crunchy, salty, plant-protein-heavy. Stays crisp 3–4 days in a jar with the lid cracked.

Ingredients to shop
  • Canned chickpeas · shelled edamame (frozen) · olive oil · smoked paprika · cayenne or chili powder · garlic powder · cumin · salt · black pepper

Snack #2 — Ultra-Creamy White Bean Hummus + Crudités

The Mediterranean Dish · Recipe → · ~2 cups dip (serves 6–8)

Tahini + butter beans. Portion into 4-oz containers Sunday night and you have an afternoon snack that's not crackers.

Ingredients to shop
  • Canned butter beans (or cannellini) · tahini · garlic · lemon · olive oil · cumin · Aleppo or red pepper flakes · salt · ice water
  • Dippers: carrots · celery · cucumber · bell pepper · cherry tomatoes · endive · sugar snap peas

SUN-5 · Radical Thinkers: Quotes & Summaries

"Leave this Europe where they are never done talking of Man, yet murder men everywhere they find them, at the corner of every one of their own streets, in all the corners of the globe. Come, then, comrades, the European game has finally ended; we must find something different."

Frantz Fanon — The Wretched of the Earth, Conclusion (1961). Marxists.org

Written from Tunisia as Algeria's independence war neared its end. Fanon was urging newly decolonizing nations not to reproduce European liberal-capitalist forms whose humanist rhetoric masks systemic violence. The passage is being re-read this year as movements for Palestinian liberation and Global South sovereignty contest a "rules-based order" whose stated values and conduct diverge sharply.

"Friedrich Engels once said: 'Bourgeois society stands at the crossroads, either transition to socialism or regression into barbarism.' … A look around us at this moment shows what the regression of bourgeois society into barbarism means. This world war is a regression into barbarism. The triumph of imperialism leads to the annihilation of civilization."

Rosa Luxemburg — The Junius Pamphlet, Ch. 1 (1915), written from a Berlin women's prison. Marxists.org

Luxemburg wrote this as the SPD voted war credits and Europe collapsed into industrialized slaughter — indicting the socialist parties that abandoned internationalism for nationalism. "Socialism or barbarism" became the touchstone of ecosocialist and anti-fascist thought, and is now being revived as climate breakdown and great-power militarism reopen the same crossroads.

"You can kill a revolutionary but you can't kill revolution. You can jail a liberator but you can't jail liberation. We're going to fight racism not with racism, but we're going to fight it with solidarity. We're not going to fight capitalism with black capitalism, but we're going to fight it with socialism."

Fred Hampton — "Power Anywhere Where There's People," Olivet Church, Chicago (1969). History Is a Weapon

Hampton was 21, chairman of the Illinois Black Panther Party, building the original Rainbow Coalition with the Young Lords and the (white Appalachian) Young Patriots. He'd be assassinated by Chicago police and the FBI months later. The speech is a touchstone for contemporary multiracial labor and tenant organizing that rejects identity-flattened "diversity" in favor of class solidarity.

"By demanding that black people repress and annihilate our rage to assimilate, to reap the benefits of material privilege in white supremacist capitalist patriarchal culture, white folks urge us to remain complicit with their efforts to colonize, oppress, and exploit."

bell hooks — Killing Rage: Ending Racism, title essay (1995). Google Books

The book opens with an airline incident that triggered a murderous rage; hooks then theorizes that rage as a legitimate political response that liberal multiculturalism pathologizes. The essay matters now as DEI rollbacks and "civility" discourse pressure marginalized people to swallow anger as the price of admission to institutions.

SUN-7 · Body & Self-Image: Weekly Wisdom

This week

The most common cognitive pattern that hijacks body-image self-talk is emotional reasoning — David Burns's classic example: "I feel fat, therefore I must be fat." Burns names it directly in Feeling Good: a feeling is being treated as evidence of reality, when it's actually a weather report from your nervous system.

The reframe isn't "love your body." It's: check the evidence you'd actually act on. Energy at 3pm. Sleep last night. Days sober. Walks taken this week. Whether you could carry the drum hardware up the stairs without stopping. That's the data. The feeling is signal, not verdict.

"I tell people that going for a run is like taking a little bit of Prozac and a little bit of Ritalin, because, like the drugs, exercise elevates these neurotransmitters."
— John Ratey, MD, Harvard Medical School, Spark. Source
"Curiosity can help you stay present in a non-judgmental way to whatever your experience is, and it's actually stronger than any type of force or willpower you might use."
— Judson Brewer, MD, PhD, Brown University. Source

The frame all week: every health move you make is for more energy, clearer thinking, less anxiety, more motivation, more enjoyment. Not appearance. Not weight loss as a destination. The destination is being able to do the things you want to do, with less drag.
