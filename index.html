SUN-1 · Sugar Addiction Recovery

Tracker data was unreachable from this run, so this section uses the general framing rather than your week's notes.

The cycle to keep naming: a sugary or refined-carb meal triggers a short blood-glucose spike, the spike triggers an insulin response, glucose crashes below baseline, and the crash shows up as fatigue, irritability, brain fog, and a fresh craving. Reaching for the next sugar hit completes the loop and trains the brain to repeat it. Anna Lembke's framing in Dopamine Nation is useful here — pleasure and pain share the same brain real estate, and every spike borrows from tomorrow's energy.

Sunday is the leverage day

Sunday is meal-prep day for a reason: most weekday sugar grabs happen because there's no easy alternative at the moment of crash. If a Tupperware of protein and fiber is already in the fridge at 3pm Tuesday, the loop doesn't get to run. The decision happens once, on Sunday, not fifty times across the week.

Three concrete moves for today

  1. Cook one protein and one fiber bulk item. A tray of Cajun salmon or a pot of red lentil dahl plus a tray of roasted vegetables covers 4–5 lunches and dinners. See SUN-4 for batch recipes. The goal isn't perfection — it's "something is ready" so future-John doesn't have to negotiate with himself at the worst possible time.
  2. Self-binding for the week. Lembke's term for putting friction between you and your drug of choice. Don't bring home the thing you can't moderate. If sweet stuff isn't in the apartment, the craving has to clear the activation energy of leaving the building — most of the time it won't.
  3. Pre-load Monday's breakfast. Protein within an hour of waking sets the day's blood-sugar rhythm. Make overnight oats with Greek yogurt and chia tonight (recipe in SUN-4); eat it before the coffee hits an empty stomach. Skipping breakfast almost guarantees a 2pm sugar grab.

Today's nudge

Don't try to break the sugar habit on Wednesday afternoon when you're crashing. Break it tonight, by making sure Wednesday afternoon you has something else to reach for. One cook session this evening is the most leveraged hour you'll spend all week — more energy Monday means more capability Monday means less reason to reach for a quick fix.

Mantra: "I'm not eating for now — I'm eating for the hour I'll need energy I don't yet have."

Sources: Anna Lembke — Dopamine Nation · Cleveland Clinic — How to Stop Sugar Cravings · Blood sugar, anxiety & the crash cycle

SUN-4 · Recipes: Meal Prep

One cook session, multiple meals. Everything below leans low/no added sugar, veggie-forward, protein-rich, and slow-carb.

Breakfast — Overnight Protein Chia Oats

Batch yield: 4 jars · 5 min hands-on, sets overnight · Meaningful Eats recipe

Ingredients (per jar — multiply ×4)
  • ⅓ cup rolled oats
  • 2 tbsp chia seeds
  • 1 scoop unflavored or vanilla whey/plant protein powder
  • ¾ cup unsweetened almond milk (or any milk)
  • 2 tbsp plain Greek yogurt (skip for dairy-free)
  • ½ tsp cinnamon, pinch of salt, splash of vanilla
  • Top day-of with berries and chopped walnuts

Dump-and-stir into 4 pint mason jars. Holds 5 days. ~30 g protein per jar without added sugar.

Lunch — Sheet-Pan Cajun Salmon with Green Beans & Baby Potatoes

Batch yield: 4 servings · 30 min total · Bites of Wellness recipe

Ingredients
  • 4 salmon fillets (~5 oz each)
  • 1.5 lb baby potatoes, halved (slow carb — fine in this amount)
  • 1 lb green beans, trimmed
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp Cajun seasoning (check label — no added sugar)
  • Salt, pepper, lemon wedges

Potatoes: 450°F for 15 min. Add seasoned salmon and beans, 12 more min. Reheats well 4 days. ~45 g protein per serving.

Dinner — Spicy Red Lentil Dahl (vegan)

Batch yield: 6 servings · 35 min · MyProtein lentil dahl

Ingredients
  • 2 cups dried red lentils, rinsed
  • 1 large yellow onion, diced
  • 4 garlic cloves + 1 tbsp grated ginger
  • 1 tbsp olive or coconut oil
  • 1 can diced tomatoes (no sugar added)
  • 1 can light coconut milk
  • 3 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 tbsp curry powder, 1 tsp turmeric, 1 tsp cumin, ½–1 tsp cayenne (to taste)
  • 1 bag baby spinach, stirred in at the end
  • Cilantro + lime to finish

Sauté aromatics, add spices for 30 sec, add liquids and lentils, simmer 20 min, fold in spinach. ~28 g plant protein per serving. Freezes well — portion 2 servings into a freezer container for week 2.

Dinner alt — Spinach & Turkey Lentil Meatballs in Curry Sauce

Batch yield: ~30 meatballs (6 meals) · 1 hr · Turkey-lentil meatballs base · Curry sauce version

Ingredients
  • 1 lb lean ground turkey
  • 1 cup cooked brown or green lentils
  • 1 cup chopped sautéed spinach + mushrooms, cooled
  • 1 egg, ¼ cup whole-wheat breadcrumbs (or almond flour)
  • 2 garlic cloves minced, 1 tsp cumin, salt/pepper
  • Curry sauce: 1 can light coconut milk, 2 tbsp red curry paste, 1 tbsp grated ginger, 1 tsp fish sauce (or tamari), juice of 1 lime

Bake meatballs 400°F for 20 min, turning halfway. Simmer sauce, add meatballs, hold 5 min. Serve over cauliflower rice or a small portion of brown rice. 4 days fridge, freezes well.

Snack 1 — Spicy Roasted Chickpeas & Edamame

Batch yield: ~6 cups (4–5 snack portions) · 25 min · This Wife Cooks recipe

Ingredients
  • 2 cans chickpeas, drained and dried well
  • 2 cups shelled edamame, thawed and dried
  • 1 tbsp olive or chili oil
  • ¾ tsp chili powder, ½ tsp smoked paprika, ½ tsp garlic powder, ¼ tsp cayenne, salt

425°F for 20 min, shake pan halfway. Best within 2 days — they soften. ~32 g protein + 20 g fiber per cup.

Snack 2 — Greek Yogurt Parfait Jars

Batch yield: 4 jars · 10 min · BREADLESS portable protein snacks

Ingredients (per jar — multiply ×4)
  • ¾ cup plain 2% Greek yogurt (no sugar)
  • 2 tbsp chopped almonds
  • ¼ cup mixed berries (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 tsp chia seeds
  • Dash of cinnamon — skip honey, the berries do the sweetening

Layer in small jars. Holds 4 days. ~18 g protein per jar, kills the 3pm crash.

SUN-5 · Radical Thinkers: Quotes & Summaries

Michael Parenti — Against Empire / Imperialism 101

"The Third World is rich. Only its people are poor — and it is because of the pillage they have endured."

Parenti's core move in Against Empire (1995) is to redirect attention from the language of "underdevelopment" to the active verbs of extraction. The Global South isn't behind; it's been systematically drained. He pairs this with a definition of imperialism worth memorizing: "the process whereby the dominant politico-economic interests of one nation expropriate for their own enrichment the land, labor, raw materials, and markets of another people." Imperialism 101.

Howard Zinn — "The Problem Is Civil Obedience" (Johns Hopkins, 1970)

"Historically, the most terrible things — war, genocide, and slavery — have resulted not from disobedience, but from obedience."

Zinn delivered this in 1970 while himself defying a court order over Vietnam-era anti-war activity. The argument inverts the usual framing: the danger isn't a citizenry that breaks the law; it's one that follows orders. Particularly worth re-reading the week before any election. Full text.

Angela Davis — Freedom Is a Constant Struggle (2016)

"Freedom can't be contained within a paradigm that is individualistic. One cannot be free alone: freedom is collective. Freedom is about transforming conditions so that communities can live in more habitable conditions."

Davis is responding here to the American tendency to flatten freedom into "I can do what I want." She's pulling it back to its older, harder meaning: a condition the most oppressed must also share, or it isn't real. Source.

Emma Goldman — Anarchism and Other Essays (1910)

"People have only as much liberty as they have the intelligence to want and the courage to take."

Goldman wrote this while still in the US, before her 1919 deportation. The line is bracing because it refuses to externalize the failure — liberty isn't bestowed by reformers, and a population that won't take it won't get it. Pair with her insistence on direct action as the consistent method. Full text.

Fred Hampton — Speech, 1969

"We say you don't fight racism with racism. We're gonna fight racism with solidarity. We say we're not going to fight capitalism with black capitalism, but we're going to fight it with socialism."

The 21-year-old chair of the Illinois Black Panthers, building the original Rainbow Coalition with poor Appalachian whites and Puerto Rican organizers months before he was murdered in his bed by Chicago police and the FBI. The two-line theory of change is still one of the most quotable distillations of multi-racial class politics. "Power Anywhere Where There's People".

SUN-7 · Body & Self-Image: Weekly Wisdom

The reframe

The CBT pattern most likely to show up around weight at 45 is emotional reasoning — "I feel sluggish, therefore I am sluggish/broken/failing" — usually layered with a touch of mind-reading ("everyone at the gig is noticing"). Both are cognitive shortcuts that feel like data. They aren't.

The honest measurement isn't the mirror or the scale. It's: How clearly did I think on Wednesday morning? How much energy did I have at the 6pm load-in? How quickly did the anxiety lift after I ate? Those are the variables that actually change life on the ground. Weight is, at most, a lagging proxy.

"Acceptance and Commitment Therapy aims to help patients accept uncomfortable internal experiences while committing to behaviors in accordance with life values." — from the NIH systematic review of ACT for body image dissatisfaction
Anna Lembke's framing is consistent: the trap isn't the body, it's the loop. "Pleasure and pain are co-located in the brain." Every short-term fix borrows from tomorrow's baseline. (Dopamine Nation)

Today's small move isn't a verdict on your body. It's a deposit on Wednesday's clarity. That's the whole metric — and it's the one a 45-year-old drummer, developer, and caterer actually needs to do the work.
