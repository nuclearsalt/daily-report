🧠 ADHD: Exercise & Mindful Eating
Body Doubling for Exercise Working out alongside someone — in person, on a video call, or even watching a livestream — creates just enough social accountability to get you moving. Your ADHD brain responds to present accountability, not future promises. A gym class, a YouTube workout-with-me video, or texting a friend "I'm going for a walk now" all count.
Dopamine-First Scheduling Rotate your exercise types to prevent the novelty crash — your brain stops releasing dopamine when the same workout becomes predictable. Add a new element every 2–3 weeks (new playlist, new route, different style). Morning exercise is particularly effective: it primes the prefrontal cortex for 4–6 hours and directly boosts dopamine and norepinephrine — the same neurotransmitters your meds target.
Mindful Eating Micro-Habit ADHD makes eating impulsive — you eat fast, don't register fullness, and hyperfocus causes missed meals followed by overeating. One fix: scoop your portion, then move the serving dish or bag out of reach. Research shows distance alone (not willpower) reduces second helpings. Prioritize protein early in the day to stabilize dopamine and reduce carb cravings later.
🟢 Today's Nudge

Before your next meal, take 3 slow breaths and sit down — even if it's just cereal. Put the box away before you start eating. That 20-second pause is the whole intervention. You don't need to eat perfectly; you need to notice you're eating.
