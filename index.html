FRI-1 · Mindful Eating & Exercise for ADHD

This week's data tells a clear story. Five of seven days logged sweets, and the reason field said "Low energy" every single time except one (and that one was "Just a craving"). On 6/23 you named the loop yourself, in your own words:

"I once again did the stupid fucking thing I always do: eat high-sugar foods. Two chocolate bars today. Cool, great. GREAT… I need to get out of The Cycle and I do not know how to make that happen."

You also wrote, the same day, that the app is helping you "maintain awareness so that I can course correct more quickly." That's not nothing. Awareness is step one. The point of today's section is to add a second tool you can reach for the next time the low-energy → sugar reach happens — because the loop is the problem, not your character.

And the data already shows the alternative exists. 6/25: "I overate at dinner, which is a bummer, but I did not eat any sweets so far today. Win!" Mood that day: 8. Energy: 7. Anxiety bumped to 6, but no sweets, and a tracker note that ends in "Win." That's a real datapoint — proof the cycle is breakable, not a slogan.

Three things to try this weekend

  1. The 10-minute energy audit before the cookie. When the "I need sugar to function" thought arrives, set a 10-minute timer. In those 10 minutes do one of: drink a full glass of water, eat 15g of protein (handful of nuts, jerky, a hard-boiled egg, a spoon of peanut butter), or step outside for 5 minutes of daylight. ADHD brains genuinely cannot distinguish "tired" from "thirsty" from "understimulated" from "low blood sugar" — the body is sending a vague alarm. The timer doesn't ban the sugar. It just buys you 10 minutes to rule out the cheaper fixes first. Often that's all it takes.
  2. Stack one healthy thing onto one thing you already do. You drummed five of the last seven days. That's a habit that's already running. Add a single "before the kit" anchor — fill a water bottle and eat a protein snack before you sit down. You don't have to remember; the drum kit reminds you. ADHD-friendly because it doesn't add a new "thing to remember," it bolts onto an existing groove.
  3. Pre-decide one weekend meal. Saturday and Sunday are decision-fatigue days. By 3pm on a busy day, your brain refuses to plan dinner and the path of least resistance wins (which usually means: nothing healthy in the fridge → chocolate-covered something). Tonight or tomorrow morning, pre-decide one specific weekend meal — pick from the FRI-2 recipes below, or repeat the tofu and garlic you already cooked Tuesday. Buy whatever ingredient is missing. Done. One meal pre-decided = one less collapse point.
Today's nudge

You named The Cycle on Tuesday. That's the part most people skip their whole lives. This weekend, the goal isn't to break it forever — it's to interrupt it once. Pick one moment, between now and Sunday night, where you'd normally reach for sugar to fix tired. Insert the 10-minute timer. Drink water, eat protein, step outside. See what happens. If the sugar still wins after 10 minutes, you've lost nothing. If it doesn't, you have a new datapoint and the cycle has a crack in it.

And remember the 6/25 win. You already did this once this week.

Mantra: Water, protein, light — then decide.

FRI-2 · Recipes: Intermediate-Level, Interesting Chefs

A day of meals from chefs working outside the mainstream blog circuit — West African, Chinese-Australian diaspora, Palestinian, Indian-American, Vietnamese-American. All low-sugar, veggie-heavy, protein-rich, slow-carb.

Breakfast — Nigerian Egg Stew (Eggs in Ata Dindin)

Yewande Komolafe · ~30 min · serves 2–3

Komolafe is the Nigerian-born NYT Cooking columnist and author of My Everyday Lagos. Ata dindin is the Yoruba bell-pepper-and-chili sauce that anchors her morning routine — bright, pepper-driven, and built around eggs.

Ingredients
  • 2 red bell peppers, roughly chopped
  • 1 large beefsteak tomato (or 2 Romas), chopped
  • 1 medium red onion, half blended, half diced
  • 1–2 habanero or scotch bonnet peppers (to taste)
  • 3 cloves garlic, 1-inch piece fresh ginger
  • 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 tsp ground crayfish or smoked paprika (optional)
  • ½ tsp curry powder, ½ tsp dried thyme
  • Salt and black pepper
  • 4–6 large eggs
  • Scallions or parsley to garnish
  • Serve with: dense seeded sourdough, boiled yam, or steel-cut oats (skip the Agege bread for slow-carb)

Lunch — Rainbow Vegetable Bowl with Harissa-Roasted Carrots, Charred Broccoli & Quinoa

Hetty Lui McKinnon · ~35 min (mostly hands-off) · serves 4

McKinnon is a Chinese-Australian, Brooklyn-based cookbook author (To Asia, With Love; Tenderheart). Plant-forward, almost no sugar, generous on herbs and textural contrast.

Ingredients
  • 2 small heads broccoli (~20 oz), cut into florets
  • 4 carrots (~14 oz), peeled and chunked
  • 1 tsp harissa flakes (or 2 tsp harissa paste)
  • Extra-virgin olive oil, sea salt, black pepper
  • 4 cups (~120g) kale leaves, torn
  • 10 oz hummus (good store-bought or homemade)
  • ½ cup mixed soft herbs — dill, mint, parsley, cilantro
  • 1 lemon (zest + juice)
  • 3–4 cups cooked quinoa, brown rice, black rice, or lentils
  • To finish: dukkah, furikake, toasted sesame, or chopped pistachios
  • Optional protein boost: chickpeas tossed with the carrots, or a soft-boiled egg per bowl

Recipe — Hetty McKinnon →

Dinner — Musakhan (Palestinian Sumac-Roasted Chicken with Caramelized Onions)

Reem Kassis · ~70 min total · serves 4

Kassis is a Jerusalem-born Palestinian author (The Palestinian Table, The Arabesque Table) who trained at Wharton and the LSE before turning to food writing. Musakhan is the recipe she most associates with her grandmother Teta Fatima; it's the national dish of Palestine.

Ingredients
  • 4 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs (or spatchcocked 3–4 lb whole chicken)
  • 4 large red onions, thinly sliced
  • ½ cup extra-virgin olive oil (heart of the dish — use a good one)
  • 3 tbsp ground sumac, divided
  • 1 tsp ground allspice, ½ tsp cinnamon, ½ tsp cumin, ½ tsp cardamom
  • Salt and black pepper
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 3 tbsp pine nuts (or slivered almonds), toasted
  • ¼ cup chopped flat-leaf parsley
  • Slow-carb serve: whole-wheat or sprouted-grain pita; cucumber-tomato-parsley salad with lemon and oil

Recipe — Saveur (Kassis-aligned) →

Snack 1 — Indian-Spiced Crispy Roasted Chickpeas

Nik Sharma · 5 min prep + 30–40 min roast · serves 2–3

Sharma is a Bombay-born, Oakland-based food scientist (The Flavor Equation, Season) who started as a molecular biologist. South Indian × American Southern with scientist's precision.

Ingredients
  • 1 (15 oz) can chickpeas, drained, rinsed, patted very dry
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive or avocado oil
  • ½ tsp each: ground cumin, ground coriander, garam masala
  • ¼ tsp ground turmeric
  • ¼ tsp Kashmiri chili powder (or cayenne)
  • ¼ tsp amchur (dried mango powder) — optional, for tang
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • Squeeze of fresh lime to finish

Nik Sharma's blog (search "roasted chickpeas") →

Snack 2 — Vietnamese Cucumber Salad with Spicy Candied Nuts (Gỏi Dưa Leo)

Andrea Nguyen · 15–20 min · serves 2

Nguyen is a Vietnamese-American author of Ever-Green Vietnamese (James Beard winner) and Vietnamese Food Any Day. Rigorously tested recipes that reduce meat and lean into vegetables without losing Vietnamese flavor logic.

Ingredients
  • 2 Persian or Japanese cucumbers, sliced on the bias
  • 1 small shallot, very thinly sliced
  • 1 small carrot, julienned
  • 1 Thai bird's-eye chili, thinly sliced (to taste)
  • ¼ cup torn cilantro and/or Vietnamese mint
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce (or Nguyen's vegan "fishless" version)
  • 1 tbsp fresh lime juice
  • 1 tsp honey or coconut sugar (minimal — for nuoc cham balance)
  • 1 small garlic clove, grated
  • ⅓ cup spicy candied peanuts or cashews
  • Optional protein: shredded poached chicken or pan-seared tofu

Andrea Nguyen's Viet World Kitchen →

FRI-6 · Shirt Shopping: John's Size & Style

Targeting chest 47" / waist 50.5" (3XL–4XL depending on brand), boho aesthetic, breathable fabrics, button-downs and V-necks both. Several retailer product pages blocked direct fetch — entries below are sourced from search results plus brand knowledge, with size-availability confidence noted per item.

  1. Men's Vintage Ethnic Boho Linen Shirt — Etsy independent seller · ~$55–$90 · Pure linen ethnic-cut button-down with a relaxed boho silhouette. Many Etsy linen sellers offer made-to-measure or extended sizes through 4XL — message the seller with your measurements (47"/50.5") before ordering. · Etsy listing
  2. Holy Clothing — Renaissance / Boho Shirts · ~$45–$75 · Brand standard is S–5X across their catalog — one of the few boho brands that genuinely carries 4X/5X. Renaissance/Celtic lace-up V-neck and pirate-shirt styles in cotton; loose, breathable, festival-ready. Highest-confidence size fit on this list. · Holy Clothing
  3. Mexicali Blues — Men's Tops · ~$40–$70 · Maine B Corp, globally sourced since 1988. Cotton gauze, rayon, hemp-blend tunics and button-ups with global/ethnic patterns — hippie-modern aesthetic in spades. Sizing typically tops out at XXL/XXXL; for 47"/50.5", look specifically at kurta-cut and tunic styles, which run roomier than fitted button-downs. · Mexicali Blues men's tops
  4. Etsy "Boho Mens V Neck Shirt" — handmade cotton gauze · ~$35–$65 · Crinkled cotton gauze V-necks — the canonical festival/hippie summer shirt. Extremely breathable, drapey, often earth tones or block prints. Many sellers list up to 4XL or offer custom sizing. · Etsy market
  5. Etsy "Bohemian Linen Shirt" — handmade button-down · ~$50–$95 · Pure linen, often unbleached or naturally dyed, with mandarin collars or open plackets. Many sellers offer made-to-measure — best route for a confirmed fit at 47"/50.5". · Etsy market
  6. Etsy "Cotton Gauze Shirt Men" · ~$40–$70 · Double-gauze cotton — the lightest, most breathable summer weave. Indian and Thai artisan sellers in this category lean heavily into kaftan-influenced silhouettes and offer 3XL/4XL or made-to-measure as standard. · Etsy market
  7. Lucky Gent — Rayon V-Neck "Boho Chic" · ~$45–$60 · Silky drapey rayon V-neck explicitly marketed as boho chic. Caveat: brand typically runs S–XXL only. Confirm 3XL availability on the product page before counting on this one. · Lucky Gent

Recommendations

  • Best guaranteed 4XL fit: Holy Clothing (S–5X is brand-standard) and Etsy made-to-measure linen sellers. These two reliably accommodate 47" chest / 50.5" waist.
  • Highest aesthetic match: Mexicali Blues kurta/tunic styles — but verify size on each item.
  • Skip unless confirmed: Lucky Gent — most "boho" brands cap at XXL.
  • Before ordering on Etsy: message the seller with chest 47" and waist 50.5" — most independent sellers will confirm fit or offer custom sizing for a small upcharge.
