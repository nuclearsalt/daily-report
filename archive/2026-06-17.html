🧠 ADHD: Exercise & Mindful Eating
72-Hour Session Spacing (New 2026 Research) A 2026 meta-analysis in Frontiers in Psychiatry found that spacing exercise sessions roughly 72 hours apart — not daily — yields the greatest gains in inhibitory control and cognitive flexibility for ADHD brains. That interval lets neurons repair and prime for the next session. Practically: 3x/week hits the sweet spot. Every day isn't better; it's counterproductive for executive function gains.
Body Doubling for Movement ADHD brains respond to present-tense accountability, not future promises. Working out alongside someone — a class, a YouTube workout-with-me video, a friend texting "I'm going for a walk" at the same time — compensates for executive function gaps that self-regulation can't bridge. Social exercise formats work precisely because your brain isn't managing initiation alone. Even a livestream counts.
Build a Dopa-Menu Beyond Food ADHD-driven impulsive eating is often dopamine-seeking — your brain wants a hit, and food is fast. The fix isn't willpower, it's competition: build a "dopa-menu" of quick dopamine sources (a specific playlist, 5 minutes of drumming, a fidget, a short walk) and reach for those before eating. Protein early in the day stabilizes dopamine and reduces carb cravings later. Put trigger foods out of direct sight — distance alone reduces consumption without any willpower required.
🟢 Today's Nudge

You don't have to go hard today. Based on the 72-hour rule, if you moved Monday or Tuesday, Wednesday is actually a rest-and-recover day that's doing neurological work. If you haven't moved yet this week: pick one thing that sounds like fun rather than exercise — drumming counts, a walk to get coffee counts. Dopamine-first, not discipline-first.
