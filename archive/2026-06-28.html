SUN-1 · Sugar Addiction Recovery

This week the tracker named the loop clearly — twice. From the 6/27 midday note: "followed that up with the chocolate covered frozen cherries because I was convinced I wouldn't have the energy to go do social things later if I didn't." From 6/28: "the chocolate covered frozen cherries, which I ate thinking they would give me sufficient energy to feel like I could make it to the jam in Harlem. They didn't." And from 6/22: "I'm in a mood. I think it's the sugar. The fucking sugar."

That's the loop the addiction-medicine literature describes: short dopamine + glucose spike → crash 60–90 min later → feeling more depleted than before → reach for the next quick hit. Dr. Anna Lembke (Stanford addiction medicine, Dopamine Nation) calls it the pleasure-pain balance: every spike is paid back by an equal dip on the other side, and the dip is what tells you to use again. The "I need energy to do the thing" thought is the cue. The cherries are the routine. The crash that follows is the reward your brain pre-registered — and it punishes the next decision.

Three things to try today (meal-prep Sunday version)

  1. Pre-decide the "low energy" snack before you need it. The cherries became the answer because no other answer was within arm's reach when the dip hit. Today, while you're prepping, batch the savory roasted chickpeas (SUN-4) and the chia jars. When 3pm fatigue arrives next Wednesday, the decision is already made — the jar is already in the fridge. The Lembke move is to design the environment so the easy option is also the right one.
  2. Name what the cherries are actually doing. Notice the cherries were never about going to the jam — they were about not feeling the fatigue. The jam was the cover story. Try the script out loud: "I'm reaching for sugar because I don't want to feel tired right now." That tiny narration breaks the automatic loop. You don't have to refuse the sugar — just notice the trade first.
  3. Protein + fat first, every meal-prepped lunch. Glucose stability is the actual lever. The chickpea-quinoa bowls and harissa-chicken sheet pan in SUN-4 are built around it: protein anchors a flat curve, slow carbs taper rather than spike, and the crash that drives the cycle never starts. The 6/25 evening note already saw it — "keep going on sweets abstention, and have my energy level out." The note is the hypothesis; the meal prep is the test.
Today's nudge

The energy you were chasing with the cherries doesn't live in the cherries. It lives in the flat blood sugar curve you're about to set up for the week. Today's prep is the energy work — every jar you batch tonight is one less 3 PM moment where the cherries are the only answer in the fridge. You already named the loop in your own words on 6/22 and again on 6/27. You're not learning this; you're acting on something you already see.

Snack 1 (savory) · Crispy Spicy Roasted Chickpeas

~4 servings (~1.5 cups) · keeps up to 2 weeks

~11g protein per serving. Shelf-stable, customizable spice level. The "instead of cherries" snack from SUN-1.

loveandlemons.com →

Ingredients
